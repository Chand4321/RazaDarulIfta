Official Legal Ruling

Use of synthetic alcohol in perfumes and its effect on Salah

ID: #8842 • Category: Taharah • Date: 12 Feb 2026
Raza Darul Ifta
wal Qaza Institution
Question
"As-salamu alaykum. I work in an environment where many perfumes contain denatured alcohol. Is it permissible to use these perfumes? Does applying them break my Wudu, or render my clothes impure (Najis) for prayer? Please clarify according to the Hanafi Madhhab."
— Questioner from London, UK
Answer
الجواب وبالله التوفيق

In the name of Allah, Most Gracious, Most Merciful.

Regarding your query, it is important to distinguish between types of alcohol. According to the Mufta-bihi (adopted) position in the Hanafi school, particularly based on the research of the later scholars (Muta'akhireen):

1. **Synthetic Alcohol:** Alcohol derived from sources other than grapes or dates (such as petroleum or synthetic sources) is not considered *Najasat-e-Ghaliza* (heavy impurity) in the same way as Khamr (wine).

2. **Permissibility:** Therefore, the use of perfumes and deodorants containing such synthetic or denatured alcohol is **permissible**. It does not render the body or clothing impure, and your Salah (prayer) performed while wearing such perfume will be valid.

3. **Wudu:** Applying perfume does not nullify the Wudu in any circumstance.

Scholarly References (Al-Maraji')

  • • Al-Durr al-Mukhtar ma’a Radd al-Muhtar (Fatawa Shami), Kitab al-Ashriba.
  • • Fatawa Hindiyyah, Vol 5, p. 412.
  • • Takmilah Fath al-Mulhim, Mufti Taqi Usmani.
والله أعلم بالصواب
